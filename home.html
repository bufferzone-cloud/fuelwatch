FuelRadar – Complete User Guide

1. Finding Fuel Stations: Use the interactive map or the search bar. Tap any station icon (green pump) on the map to see distance, price, amenities, and status.

2. Navigating to a Station: Click the "Navigate" button on a station's popup or card. The app will calculate the fuel needed based on your active vehicle's consumption and draw a route on the map.

3. Trip Planner: A powerful tool for road trips.

  • Step 1: Enter your destination. The app suggests places based on your location.
  • Step 2: Choose a fuel station along your route. The app automatically finds stations on the way.
  • Step 3: Pick fun stops (restaurants, attractions, parks, lakes, monuments, museums, malls) to make the journey enjoyable.
  • Step 4: Review your trip summary, estimated costs, and safety tips. Click "Draw Route on Map" to visualize the entire journey with color-coded segments.

4. My Garage: Add your vehicles by providing name, license plate, tank capacity, consumption (L/100km), and fuel type. Upload photos of your car (Front, Back, Side) using the horizontal boxes. Track your refueling history and view consumption trends on a chart. Set an active vehicle to get accurate trip calculations.

5. Fuel Calculator: Enter a trip distance, and the app calculates the required fuel, cost, and estimated time of arrival (ETA) based on your active vehicle's consumption and current fuel prices.

6. AI Chat: Ask FuelRadar AI anything about fuel, routes, vehicle maintenance, or road trips. The AI can provide recommendations, troubleshooting tips, and general driving advice.

7. Reporting a Station: If you notice incorrect prices, out-of-stock issues, or closed stations, use the "Report" button on any station card or popup. Your reports help the community.

8. Favorites & History: Save your favorite stations for quick access. View your past trips and recently visited stations.

9. Dark Mode: Toggle Dark Mode in the main menu (☰) for a comfortable night-time driving experience.

10. Data Export: Export your fuel logs, favorites, reports, and trip history as CSV or JSON files for backup or analysis.
